Who am I?

I am dedicated to investigating and addressing a wide range of challenges in both the social, engineering and environmental domains. In the social engineering realm, I am deeply interested in tackling issues such as traffic congestion, smart cities, healthy food siting intervention, pothole identification and mapping using ML/AI, crime hotspot analysis, urban heat islands, and infectious disease analysis. Through these endeavors, I aim to develop innovative solutions for efficient transportation, sustainable urban living, improved community well-being, enhanced infrastructure maintenance, public safety, sustainable urban environments, and effective disease control and prevention.

Simultaneously, within the environmental domain, I focus on understanding and mitigating various environmental challenges. These include coastal erosion, forest biomass dynamics, benthic coral quantification and bleaching, landslide susceptibility, and flood modeling. By studying these areas, I strive to develop sustainable approaches that contribute to the preservation and sustainable management of natural resources, such as mitigating the impact of coastal erosion, optimizing forest management, conserving coral reefs, improving landslide susceptibility assessments, and developing accurate flood models for disaster preparedness and community protection. Overall, my work spans both social, engineering and environmental spheres, aiming to make a positive impact on communities and the environment alike.

Automation of 3D buildings from LiDAR

Automating the extraction and visualisation of airborne LiDAR point clouds into 3D parametric surfaces. This was exploratory in nature and a small subset of the point clouds over the university area was used. Coding was performed usin the R computing language.

Movement Economy using Space Syntax Methodology

Space syntax is a set of theories and statistical methods that are used to model and analyse movement within cities. This small project applies that theory to model and analyse the street network of Port Moresby in Papua New Guinea* within a 10km radius from the CBD.

Predicting Carbon Stocking Using LiDAR Point Clouds & Field Plot Measurements

This study endeavours to identify the spatial variations in above ground biomass within forest plantations using airborne LiDAR derived point clouds and to compare that with the NZ ETS look-up tables.
